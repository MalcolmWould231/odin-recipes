Baked Crab Legs with Old Bay Seasoning

Description

This recipe brings the best out of crab legs with the bold flavors of Old Bay seasoning and a touch of garlic and lemon zest. Baking the crab legs ensures they stay moist and flavorful, while the Old Bay seasoning adds a delightful, spicy kick. This dish is perfect for an easy, yet impressive, seafood dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of crab legs (king crab or snow crab)
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tablespoon of Old Bay seasoning
    • 3
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of lemon zest
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven:
    • Preheat your overn to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the Crab Legs:
    • Place the crab legs on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil. If the crab legs are too large, you may need to break them into smaller sections to fit.
  3. Season the Crab Legs:
    • In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, Old Bay seasoning, garlic powder, and lemon zest. Mix well.
    • Brush the butter mixture evenly over the crab legs, ensuring they are well-coated.
  4. Bake:
    • Cover the crab legs with another piece of aluminum foil to keep them from drying out while baking.
    • Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 20 minutes. The foil cover will help steam the crab legs slightly, keeping them tender and moist.
  5. Serve:
    • Remove the crab legs from the oven and carefully discard the top layer of foil.
    • Transfer the crab legs to a serving platter.
    • Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of color and freshness.
    • Serve immediately with lemon wedges on the side for squeezing over the crab meat.
